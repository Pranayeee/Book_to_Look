Background

The Notebook was Nicholas Sparks' first published novel and written over a time period of six months in 1994.Literary agent Theresa Park discovered Sparks by picking the book out of her agency's slush pile and reading it. Park offered to represent him. In October 1995, Park secured a $1 million advance for the book from the Time Warner Book Group, and the novel was published in October 1996. It was on The New York Times Best Seller list in its first week of release. The Notebook was a hardcover best seller for more than a year. In interviews, Sparks said he was inspired to write the novel by the story of his wife's grandparents, who had been married for more than 60 years when he met them. In The Notebook, he tried to express the long romantic love of that couple.

SUMMARY

"The Notebook" is a touching love story that begins with Noah Calhoun, an elderly man, reading a tale to a woman in a nursing home. The story he shares recounts his own life and romance with Allie, a woman he fell in love with during a passionate summer in their youth. After being separated for seven years due to class differences and her family's interference, including her mother hiding Noah's letters, they reconnect when Allie visits him in New Bern, North Carolina. Although she is engaged to Lon, her feelings for Noah resurface. Over several heartfelt days, they share memories, a canoe ride on a serene lake, and eventually, a night of intimacy. Torn between Noah and Lon, Allie initially decides to leave, but not without the weight of her rekindled love for Noah. It is later revealed that the elderly man reading the story is Noah himself, and the woman is Allie, who no longer remembers him due to Alzheimer’s disease. Despite his own failing health, Noah is devoted to her, recalling their life together—her career as a painter, their family, and the challenges of growing old. In rare moments of clarity, Allie recognizes Noah, and they share brief but profound expressions of love before her memory fades again. In the story’s poignant conclusion, after recovering from a stroke, Noah sneaks into Allie’s room at night. She awakens, recognizes him, and they fall asleep in each other’s arms, believing in the enduring power of their love. This emotional narrative explores themes of devotion, memory, and the timeless strength of true love.
